
**About**

I am a PhD student in [History and Philosophy of Science at University of Pittsburgh](https://www.hps.pitt.edu/).

I work on philosophy of science and AI, especially epistemological and ethical questions that arise when AI and automation are integrated into scientific practice.

Over Summer 2026, I participated in the Northeastern Philosophy Department's [AI + Data Ethics (AIDE) Summer Program](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/ethics/aide-summer/).

I am also a Graduate Student Awardee at the University of Pittsburgh's [Hub for AI and Data Science Leadership (HAIL)](https://www.aihub.pitt.edu/). You can find a short profile written about my participation [here](https://www.aihub.pitt.edu/news/hail-graduate-awardees-spotlight-rose-gatfield-jeffries).

Before studying at Pittsburgh, I completed an MPhil in the History and Philosophy of Science, Medicine, and Technology at University of Cambridge. Before that I studied History and Philosophy of Science and Political Economy at University of Sydney. I was born and raised in Aotearoa New Zealand.
