## Instructor of Record

**Thinking About the Environment**, *University of Pittsburgh Spring 2027*

Develops the analytical tools to reason clearly about pressing environmental
issues (e.g., climate change, nuclear power, and chemical pesticides)
and encourages students to find the best available evidence and judge what
conclusions, and what actions, it warrants.

**Science in Global Perspective**, *University of Pittsburgh Fall 2026*

An examination of science as a global, cross-cultural enterprise: how
scientific knowledge travels across societies and is shaped by different
cultural, political, and historical contexts.

## Teaching Assistant

**Mind and Medicine**, *University of Pittsburgh Fall 2024; Spring 2025*

Explores fundamental philosophical questions about the nature of mental health
and mental illness, and about the branches of medicine and psychiatry that aim
to promote mental health and treat mental illness.

**Science Towards the Future**, *University of Sydney Spring 2020; Summer 2021; Fall 2022*

Considers how science tackles the complex, interdisciplinary problems of the
twenty-first century: from climate change to disease outbreaks, and how to
create and communicate scientific evidence within its ethical, social, and
political contexts.

**Science, Ethics, and Society**, *University of Sydney Spring 2020*

Introduces the ethical dimensions of scientific research, using contemporary
and historical case studies to analyse the social and ethical issues raised by
research involving humans and animals, and the codes of conduct that govern it.
